An air of unreality

February 23 2010, 6:41 AM  by Brian Condon
There's an air of unreality about my preparations to go on the tuttle2texas trip. Apart from the obvious problem that I'm not actually going to make it as far as Texas as I will return from New Orleans (via Toronto!) on 13th March.

I've been focused on trying to get the Centre for Creative Collaboration up and running (www.creativecollaboration.org.uk) with my collleagues Thias Martin and Neil Gregory; trying to get the internet into the space (do you realise that you can't actually call BT to order a business phone line - you have to do it all online); working with Lloyd and meeting lots of people who want to visit Acton Street and trying to keep other stuff moving.

The main reason for my trip to North America is to go to see the MaRS Discovery District in Toronto (http://www.marsdd.com/aboutmars/mission) which combines innovation and social entrepreneurship. I've studied this project from afar and heard about it from people who've been there; but this is the first time I'll be seeing it. I'm also organising other meetings in Toronto.

Then, on Saturday, I'm taking the train to the USA - an overnight stop in Albany; then another train to Boston. Why the train? Why not?

To add to the air of unreality, when I should probably be at home getting sorted for the trip; I'm going to be at the tuttle2texas fund-raiser this evening. See you there!
