Nailing down the itinerary Part 1

January 28 2010, 3:38 PM  by Lloyd Davis

Right.  Let's come at it from the other end.

We want to be in Austin late on 11th or early on 12th March for the opening.

This means leaving New Orleans on the 11th - it's an 8 or 9 hour drive (Taylor has offered a lift & there may be a bus going) so leaving early on the 11th feels do-able.  I had a lingering idea that I'd love to actually roll in to Austin station - I saw it last year and parked it as part of the idea that turned into this year's trip.  However, New Orleans has become a solid part of the trip too and insanely enough the timetable suggests that the only way from NOLA to Austin is via (wait for it...) Chicago and if we left NOLA on Thursday 11th, we'd get to Austin on the Saturday.  So it's all working out beautifully.  I really want to have a full day in New Orleans so that would be the 10th and so we need to arrive on 9th.

So the 9th is a travel day from Atlanta to NOLA leaving Atlanta at 08:38 and arriving in the Big Easy at 19:38 yup a cool 12-hour trip.

Now the trip from Washington to Atlanta is a biggie too - 14 hours overnight.  So to be able to leave on the 9th we'll have to arrive on the morning of 8th which means leaving Washington on Sunday night (7th) Train leaves Union Station at 18:30

Our time in Washington then would seem to be Saturday night and Sunday.  We could leave New York on Saturday morning and get to Washington after lunch in time for an evening do and then maybe a brunch date on Sunday.

This means our main event will need to be on Friday evening in New York and to make the most of our time there we should arrive on the afternoon of 4th.  Boston to NYC is 4 hours - so leaving first thing (there's an 09:40 gets in at 13:50) should get us there OK.

This means an evening event on 3rd in Boston and probably arriving the night before in order to give time for last minute glitches.

Oh, so that means leaving UK on 2nd - earlier than I expected.  Which has implications for costs - I just added 3 nights accomodation to the budget... ah well, better to know now.  In my head, I now have every project manager I've ever worked with looking at me over the top of their glasses, nodding and saying "I told you so"

so to summarize in reverse order, which is actually the right order:

2nd March Fly London to Boston

3rd March day in Boston for conversations (any Berkman Centre folk up for that?) and a Boston Tuttle in the evening.

4th March 09:40 to NYC arrives 13:50 evening tweetup

5th March day in NYC for conversations and exploration (New Work City and other coworkers?) unconfirmed sponsor event in the evening

6th March 10:05 to Washington, DC arrives 13:25 evening conversation space and tweetup

7th March Brunch conversation spaces, afternoon exploration 18:30 to Atlanta arrives 08:12 the next day

8th March day in Atlanta, conversations, exploration, evening tweetup

9th March 08:38 to New Orleans arrive 19:38

10th March day in NOLA - exploration & conversations in the hands of Mr Taylor Davidson

11th March drive with Taylor to Austin

12th SXSWi begins - sighs of relief all round
